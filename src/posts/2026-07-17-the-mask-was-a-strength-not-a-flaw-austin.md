There's a version of this conversation that goes wrong, and I want to name it before someone in Austin has it with themselves tonight.

It goes: masking is inauthentic, you've been living a lie, the real you has been buried all along, tear it all down. That framing feels dramatic and true for about a week. Then it curdles into shame, and shame is the one thing that never got anyone out of a mask.

Here's the version that actually works.

## First, give the mask credit

If you built your mask young, around a parent or sibling whose ego ran the house, then that mask did its job. It got you through years you didn't choose, in a room you couldn't leave. It kept a small person safe until she was big enough to walk out.

Then it kept working. The same moves that managed a volatile home turned out to be worth money: sensing what the client won't say, staying flawless under a hostile partner's questions, making every stakeholder feel handled. Plenty of the most capable operators I've met, in Austin and everywhere else, are running skills they built at eight years old.

So say the true thing first: the mask was a strength. You were never weak for wearing it. You were smart, early, under conditions that gave you no better option.

## Then notice what it costs now

A survival skill that never turns off stops being a skill and becomes a tax. It runs during meetings where nobody is dangerous. It runs at dinner with people who would love the unperformed version of you. It runs when you're alone, which is the strangest and most telling place to catch it.

And it burns real energy. If you're also managing chronic illness, or a nervous system that was doing extra work long before anyone noticed, that's energy you cannot afford to spend on a threat that no longer exists.

## The goal is choice, not demolition

You don't have to become someone who can't do polish. The polish is yours, you paid for it. The change worth making is smaller and harder: adding an off switch.

An off switch needs somewhere safe to practice. That's the actual reason [the gathering](/events/) exists: one table, downtown, where every person sitting at it also knows exactly what a mask weighs. Nobody there needs your performance, and nobody mistakes dropping it for weakness, because they know what building it took.

The mask got you here. It doesn't have to run the rest of your life. [Request a seat](/events/) and try one evening with it off.
