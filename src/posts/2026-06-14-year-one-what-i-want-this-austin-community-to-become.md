I want to end this first stretch of writing where a community should always start: honestly, out loud, about what I'm actually trying to build. Consider this the founding document. If you're reading it early, you're not a spectator, you're a potential co-builder, and I mean that literally.

## Where this starts

Right now, [Built & Unwell](/about/) is two things: this blog, in my real voice, and a [monthly gathering in downtown Austin](/events/) built for real bodies. That's it, and that's on purpose. I'm not interested in a big empty launch. I'm interested in the *right people*, accomplished founders and operators in Austin who live with chronic illness and are tired of having no room that fits both halves of who they are.

Year one is not a numbers game. I would rather have twenty people who genuinely belong than two thousand who don't. [Depth over breadth](/blog/where-chronically-ill-founders-meet-downtown-austin/) isn't just my networking advice; it's the whole design principle of this community. The strongest networks (the [First Round](https://review.firstround.com/) communities, the real ones) were never built on size. They were built on trust and fit, slowly.

## Where it's going

Here's the honest vision. I want this to become the professional home this group has never had. Concretely, over time:

- **The gathering deepens**, from a monthly table into a real, trusted circle where warm intros, referrals, and genuine help move between people who've earned each other's trust.
- **The writing compounds**, a growing library that says the things nobody else is saying about [building while chronically ill](/blog/hidden-tax-chronic-illness-building-business-austin/), so people searching in the dark find their people faster than I did.
- **The model travels**, if this works in Austin, the same room should exist in other cities. Austin first, because it's home and because you build community where you actually live. But the need isn't local, and I know it.

I'm holding that vision loosely on the details and tightly on the core: accomplished, chronically ill, peer to peer, never medical, rooted in a real place.

> I'm not building an audience. I'm building a room. The difference is that in a room, everyone knows your name, and you help decide what it becomes.

## How you help build it

This is being built in the open, and the earliest people shape it more than anyone who comes later ever will. So:

- **Introduce yourself.** [Tell me who you are](/community/) and what you've built. The community is quite literally its members.
- **Come downtown.** [Show up to a gathering.](/events/) Be one of the faces that sets the tone.
- **Pass it on.** If you know the woman I keep [describing](/blog/accomplished-women-chronically-ill-austin/) (accomplished, quietly running something serious, privately unwell) send her here.

I couldn't find this room in Austin, so I'm building it. A year from now I want to look back at this post and see how much of it we made real together. [Come help me.](/community/)
