I'll say the true thing first, because that's the whole point of this place: I've built things I'm proud of, and I'm also chronically ill. Both are true at once, every single day, and almost no room I walk into is built for both.

## Why "The Understory"

In a forest, the understory is the layer that grows beneath the canopy — in the shade, out of the light, doing the quiet structural work that holds the whole system up. It's easy to miss and impossible to do without. That's us: people producing at a high level under conditions nobody can see, rarely getting credit for how much harder the same output costs. This is a room for the understory.

## The two rooms that don't fit

Walk into a typical **entrepreneur** community and the unspoken assumption is a body that always shows up — early mornings, late networking, "just push through." Many of the people there are running solo shops, and there's nothing wrong with that, but it's not the room I'm looking for. I came up through serious work, and I want to be around people who have run a real P&L, managed managers, sat in the rooms at top companies, done the consulting and finance grind — people who already built something and have the scars to show for it.

Walk into a typical **chronic illness** space and you're a patient first. The conversation is symptoms and systems and coping. Necessary — but it treats the most ambitious part of you like it's beside the point.

I needed a third room. So I'm building it.

## What this is

**[The Understory](/) is a network for accomplished people in Austin who run businesses while living with chronic illness.** It's a blog in my own voice, a monthly gathering in [downtown Austin](/events/), and a growing, private-feeling group of people who get both halves of that sentence — the achievement and the illness — without needing either explained.

It is **not a medical website.** Let me be blunt about why. A lot of us — me included — have been burned by medical providers. Dismissed, mislabeled, billed, and sent away worse than we came. So when I want real help building a life and a business around an unpredictable body, the last place I'm going is a site written by the same institutions that failed me. This is peer to peer. Lived experience, not a clinic.

## Who I'm looking for

- You've **built something real** — a company, a team, a P&L, a hard-won career.
- You came up through **serious work**: top-50 corporate leadership, strategy consulting, investment banking or high finance, or your own venture.
- You live with **chronic illness** and you're done pretending you don't.
- You're in **Austin** — ideally close enough to make it downtown now and then.

Many of the people I most want in the room are women. Men who fit the rest absolutely belong too.

## Why Austin, why downtown

Because this is where I live, and community is a local thing before it's anything else. I want people I can actually sit across from — in a quiet booth off Congress, a back table where nobody's rushing me out. Every post on this site is rooted in Austin on purpose. If you're somewhere else and you wish this existed near you, [tell me](/community/) — that's useful information.

If any of this landed, [join the network](/community/). Come help me build the room I couldn't find.
