I'll say the true thing first, because that's the whole point of this place: I've built things I'm proud of, and I'm also chronically ill. Both are true at once, every single day, and almost no room I walk into is built for both.

## Why "Built & Unmasking"

The name is the whole thesis in two words. **Built**: because everyone here has already done the hard thing: built a company, a career, a P&L, a team. **Unmasking**: because what you're carrying, chronic illness, high-functioning autism, or something else, gets performed away every day so nobody has to see it. Not "unwell." Autism isn't an illness, and I'm not going to name a room after a word that implies it is. Not "seen" either. Whether anyone out there notices what you're carrying was never something you got to control. Unmasking is the part that's actually yours: the choice to stop performing fine, in a room full of people who already get it. Autism and chronic illness are different mechanisms, but they use the same word for the same cost, so this name doesn't ask anyone to pick a side of that to belong here.

## The two rooms that don't fit

Walk into a typical **entrepreneur** community and the unspoken assumption is a body that always shows up: early mornings, late networking, "just push through." Many of the people there are running solo shops, and there's nothing wrong with that, but it's not the room I'm looking for. I came up through serious work, and I want to be around people who have run a real P&L, managed managers, sat in the rooms at top companies, done the consulting and finance grind, people who already built something and have the scars to show for it.

Walk into a typical **chronic illness** space and you're a patient first. The conversation is symptoms and systems and coping. Necessary, but it treats the most ambitious part of you like it's beside the point.

I needed a third room. So I'm building it.

## The pattern underneath

Nearly 80% of autoimmune disease patients are women. That's not a coincidence. It's a pattern a lot of us live with: something real happening in the body, invisible to everyone around you, while the world expects you to show up flawless anyway.

High-functioning autism fits the same pattern, just less obviously. People don't expect autism to look like a woman running a company, so a lot of us go undiagnosed for decades: we just get very good at masking. I didn't know how much I was masking until I spent a week not doing it. When I went back to talking to the same people, doing the same normal interactions, I felt exactly how much it had been costing me the whole time.

That's the real thesis of this place. Not illness, and not autism specifically: invisible difference, under high social expectation to hide it, carried mostly by women. Chronic illness and high-functioning autism are two different mechanisms landing in the same spot.

## What this is

**[Built & Unmasking](/) is a network for accomplished people in Austin who run businesses while living with chronic illness.** The center of it is a monthly gathering in [downtown Austin](/events/), a table where people say the real thing instead of the polished one. The blog is here too, in my own voice, but the room is where it actually happens: people who get both halves of that sentence (the achievement and the illness) without needing either explained.

It is **not a medical website.** Let me be blunt about why. A lot of us (me included) have been burned by medical providers. Dismissed, mislabeled, billed, and sent away worse than we came. So when I want real help building a life and a business around an unpredictable body, the last place I'm going is a site written by the same institutions that failed me. This is peer to peer. Lived experience, not a clinic.

## How I actually keep both running

Energy is the real constraint, not time. So I built AI agents to track my own doctor's appointments and medications, and other agents to run pieces of my team, because I don't have spare energy to burn on remembering and repeating. That's not a tip I'm handing you, it's just how I keep the business and the body both moving without either one eating the other.

## Who ends up in the room

Mostly it's people who came up through top-50 corporate roles, strategy consulting, investment banking, or built their own company from nothing, and who are also carrying chronic illness, high-functioning autism, or something else no one can see. A lot of us are women. Men who fit that same shape are welcome too. Nobody's checking credentials at the door, that's just who tends to walk in.

## Why Austin, why downtown

Because this is where I live, and community is a local thing before it's anything else. I want people I can actually sit across from, in a quiet booth off Congress, a back table where nobody's rushing me out. Every post on this site is rooted in Austin on purpose.

If any of this landed, don't email anyone about it. [Come to the next gathering](/events/), sit down, and say the real thing to people who already get it. That's the whole ask.
